Nikki Bella is a professional wrestler, model, actress, and entrepreneur who has a net worth of a staggering $8 million as of 2023.

Stephanie Nicole Garcia-Colace, famously known as Nikki Bella, is known for her wrestling career on WWE’s Smackdown brand.

Nikki Bella was born in San Diego, California, on November 21, 1983. She was born 16 minutes before her twin sister Brie Bella

Bella debuted on Smackdown in 2008. Together with her sister Brie Bella, she formed one-half of the wrestling duo known as The Bella Twins.

She eventually became the two-time WWE Divas Champion after debuting for “Smackdown” in 2008.

Nikki Bella holds the record for the longest reign as WWE Divas Champion, holding the championship for 301 days.

In 2016, Nikki and her sister, Brie, shared the Teen Choice Award for Choice Female Athlete.

After graduation, she started modeling, acting, and doing promotional work.

She made her TV debut with the Fox reality show Meet My Folks.

Following their appearance, the Garcia twins were appointed as Budweiser’s World Cup Twins and were seen with the World Cup trophy.

She dated and was even engaged to the fellow WWE superstar John Cena, but they called off the engagement and canceled the wedding for obscure reasons.

Nikki Bella | Career

Bella began her career by hunting for jobs in modeling and acting, and she eventually got a head start by appearing in a Fox TV show, ‘Meet my Folks.’

Nikki and her twin sister were signed to a development contract by WWE in June 2007, and they made their in-ring debut in the Florida Championship wrestling on September 15, 2007.

She won her first WWE Divas Championship after defeating Beth Phoenix on Raw in 2012.

However, her reign as champion was brief, as she was defeated one week later by Layla.

At Summerslam 2014, Nikki turned heel after attacking her twin sister, Brie Bella, during her match with Stephanie McMahon.

Nikki Bella won her second Diva’s title, Survivor Series 2014, when she defeated A.J. Mendez, which she held for the longest period of 301 days.

Nikki retired from professional wrestling on June 20, 2019, as doctors found that she had a cyst on her brain.

Nikki and her twin sister Brie were inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame on February 21, 2020, broadcast of SmackDown.
