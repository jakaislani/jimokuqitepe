Who is Jörg Schmadtke?

German football manager, Jörg Schmadtke was born on March 16, 1964 in Düsseldorf, West Germany.

After serving as Vorstand sport at 1. FC Köln, the former goalkeeper and coach now serves as managing director of sport for VFL Wolfsburg.

He participated in 266 Bundesliga games with Fortuna Düsseldorf (1990–1993), SC Freiburg (1993–1997), and Bayer 04 Leverkusen (1997–1998).

Schmadtke helped a club significantly improve its financial and athletic circumstances three times.

Jörg Schmadtke career

Schmadtke began his position as sporting director at Aachen in December 2001. The team had a four million euro deficit and was in risk of losing its license.

He was successful in strengthening the team and scouting. Alemannia Aachen advanced to the German Cup final in the 2004–05 season.

The squad was promoted to the top flight for the ensuing season. The club enjoyed financial success. He announced in October 2008 that he would not be renewing his contract, which was valid until 2009, and was fired the next day.

Schmadtke reportedly knew every player from the first to the fourth tier, according to coach Dieter Hecking. Erik Meijer, Simon Rolfes, Jan Schlaudraff, and Vedad Ibisevic were among those that were transferred.

Schmadtke served as Hannover 96’s sporting director from the summer of 2009 to the month of June 2013. He was given an indeterminate contract in 2011 and was appointed “Geschäftsführer Sport” to the executive board.

Hannover had a few very successful seasons, both financially and athletically (they finished fourth in the league and participated in the UEFA Cup).

In 2012, Schmadtke took a break from work and reduced his workload for personal reasons. He requested a contract termination in April 2013.

Didier Ya Konan, Mohammed Abdellaoue, Lars Stindl, Emanuel Pogatetz, Ron-Robert Zieler, and Mame Diouf were among those that were transferred.

He briefly discussed working with Hamburger SV before beginning his position as Co-CEO Sports at 1. FC Köln Ltd. in June 2013. After achieving promotion to the Bundesliga, Köln has consistently improved its athletic and financial standing.

A contract extension until 2023 was signed by Schmadtke and Wehrle in April 2017. Dominique Heintz, Anthony Modeste, Leonardo Bittencourt, Marco Höger, and Jorge Meré were among the transfers.

Schmadtke won “Manager of the Year” honors in 2011 and 2017. He left on October 23, 2017.

Schmadtke was hired by VfL Wolfsburg as the organization’s new Director of Sport on May 22, 2018, according to a tweet from the team.

He was supposed to begin on July 1, but on June 1, VfL Wolfsburg announced on its website that he could begin right away because of a deal reached between 1. FC Köln and VfL Wolfsburg.

According to newspaper reports, Wolfsburg paid Köln 500,000 Euros to overturn the occupational prohibition placed on Schmadtke, allowing him to start working one month earlier than anticipated.
